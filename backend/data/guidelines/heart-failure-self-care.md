# Living With Heart Failure

Heart failure means the heart cannot pump enough blood to meet the body's needs.
It is a long-term condition, but daily self-care can reduce symptoms and help you
avoid hospital stays.

Take all heart failure medicines exactly as prescribed, including diuretics
(water pills), ACE inhibitors or ARBs, and beta blockers. Do not stop a medicine
or change the dose without talking to your care team.

Weigh yourself at the same time every morning, after using the bathroom and
before eating. A sudden weight gain of 2 to 3 pounds in a day or about 5 pounds
in a week can mean your body is holding extra fluid and should be reported to
your care team.

Limit sodium (salt) because it makes the body hold fluid. Many people are advised
to stay under about 2 grams of sodium per day. Your team may also ask you to
limit how much liquid you drink.

Call your care team if you notice more shortness of breath, swelling in the legs
or abdomen, needing extra pillows to sleep, or a new cough. Get emergency help
for chest pain, severe trouble breathing, fainting, or a fast or irregular
heartbeat that does not go away.
