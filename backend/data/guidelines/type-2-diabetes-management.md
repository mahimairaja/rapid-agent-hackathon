# Managing Type 2 Diabetes

Managing diabetes means keeping blood glucose, blood pressure, and cholesterol in
healthy ranges through daily habits and, when prescribed, medicine.

If you take metformin, take it with food to lower the chance of stomach upset.
Mild nausea or loose stools when starting are common and usually improve. Take
medicines at the same time each day.

Eat balanced meals with vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains, and limit
sugary drinks and large portions of refined carbohydrates. Regular physical
activity, such as 30 minutes of walking on most days, helps lower blood glucose.

If you check your blood sugar, keep a log of the readings and bring it to
appointments. Ask your team for your target range.

Know the signs of low blood sugar (shakiness, sweating, confusion) and treat it
with a fast-acting sugar such as juice or glucose tablets. Call your care team if
readings are often very high or very low. Seek emergency care for vomiting that
prevents keeping fluids down, deep rapid breathing, fruity-smelling breath, or
confusion, which can signal a serious problem.
