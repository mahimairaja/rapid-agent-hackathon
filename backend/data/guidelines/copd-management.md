# Living With COPD

Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) is a long-term lung disease that
makes breathing harder over time. A daily plan helps you stay active and avoid
flare-ups.

Use your inhalers as prescribed. Long-acting maintenance inhalers are taken every
day, even when you feel well, to keep the airways open. A short-acting rescue
inhaler is used for sudden shortness of breath. Rinse your mouth after using
inhaled medicines.

Avoid triggers such as tobacco smoke, strong fumes, and air pollution. If you
smoke, quitting is the most important step to slow the disease. Use pursed-lip
breathing and pace activities with rest breaks.

Watch for signs of a flare-up: more shortness of breath than usual, more coughing,
or a change in the amount or color of mucus to yellow or green. Contact your care
team early, because flare-ups are easier to treat when caught quickly.

Get emergency help if you have severe shortness of breath that does not improve
with your rescue inhaler, blue lips or fingertips, confusion, or chest pain.
