# Preventing Blood Clots After Surgery

After surgery, especially orthopedic surgery such as a knee or hip replacement,
you are at higher risk for blood clots in the legs (deep vein thrombosis) that can
travel to the lungs (pulmonary embolism). Following your prevention plan lowers
this risk.

Take any blood-clot prevention medicine exactly as prescribed. This may be a
low-dose aspirin or another blood thinner for a set number of weeks. Do not stop
early without asking your care team.

Move as directed. Get up and walk short distances as soon as it is safe, do the
ankle and leg exercises you were taught, and wear compression stockings if they
were prescribed. Avoid sitting or lying still for long periods.

Care for your surgical site by keeping the dressing clean and dry and following
instructions about showering. Do your physical therapy exercises and use ice for
swelling as directed.

Call your care team for increasing redness, warmth, or drainage at the incision,
or a fever. Seek emergency care for pain or swelling in one calf, or for sudden
shortness of breath or chest pain, which can be signs of a blood clot in the lung.
