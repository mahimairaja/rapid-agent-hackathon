Aisha Patel is a 35-year-old woman seen for newly diagnosed type 2 diabetes after presenting with increased thirst and fatigue. She is medically stable and is being started on lifestyle changes and a single oral medication.

## Diagnoses
- Type 2 diabetes mellitus, newly diagnosed

## Medications at discharge
- Metformin 500 mg by mouth once daily with the evening meal

Take metformin with food to reduce stomach upset. Mild nausea or loose stools in the first week are common and usually improve.

## Daily self-care
Aim for balanced meals, limit sugary drinks, and try to be active for 30 minutes most days. If you were given a glucose meter, check your fasting blood sugar in the morning and keep a log to review at your appointments.

## Warning signs: when to get help
Call your care team if your blood sugar readings are repeatedly above 250 mg/dL, or if you have symptoms of low blood sugar such as shakiness, sweating, or confusion. Treat low blood sugar with a fast-acting sugar such as juice.

Seek emergency care if you have severe symptoms such as vomiting that prevents you from keeping fluids down, deep rapid breathing, fruity-smelling breath, or confusion.
