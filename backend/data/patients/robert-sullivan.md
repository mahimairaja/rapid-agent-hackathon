# Discharge Summary: Robert Sullivan

Robert Sullivan is a 71-year-old man discharged after an exacerbation of chronic
obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). His breathing has improved with inhalers
and a short course of steroids, and his oxygen levels are stable on room air.

## Diagnoses
- Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease with acute exacerbation

## Medications at discharge
- Albuterol inhaler, 2 puffs every 4 to 6 hours as needed for shortness of breath
- Tiotropium 18 mcg inhaled once daily, every day, to keep the airways open
- Prednisone 20 mg by mouth once daily, finishing the course on June 16

Use the tiotropium every day even when you feel well. Rinse your mouth after
inhaled medicines. Do not stop the prednisone early unless told to.

## Daily self-care
Avoid smoke and strong fumes. Use pursed-lip breathing when short of breath and
pace your activities with rest breaks. If you use home oxygen, follow the
prescribed flow rate.

## Warning signs: when to get help
Call your care team if you notice more shortness of breath than usual, a change
in the color or amount of your mucus to yellow or green, or a fever. These can
be early signs of a flare-up.

Call 911 if you have severe shortness of breath that does not improve with your
rescue inhaler, blue lips or fingertips, confusion, or chest pain.
