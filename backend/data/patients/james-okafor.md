# Discharge Summary: James Okafor

James Okafor is a 53-year-old man discharged on the first day after an elective right total knee replacement. Surgery and recovery were uncomplicated. He is walking short distances with a walker and his pain is controlled.

## Procedure
- Right total knee arthroplasty

## Medications at discharge
- Oxycodone 5 mg by mouth every 6 hours as needed for pain, for up to one week
- Acetaminophen 500 mg by mouth every 6 hours as needed for pain
- Aspirin 81 mg by mouth once daily for 4 weeks to prevent blood clots

Use the oxycodone only when you need it and stop as soon as acetaminophen alone controls the pain. Do not drink alcohol while taking these medicines.

## Wound and activity care
Keep the surgical dressing clean and dry. You may shower after 48 hours but do not soak the knee in a bath or pool until cleared. Do your physical therapy exercises every day and use ice for swelling. Wear the compression stockings as directed.

## Warning signs: when to get help
Call the orthopedic office if you notice:
- Increasing redness, warmth, or drainage from the incision, or a fever over 101 F
- Pain not controlled by your medicines

Call 911 or go to the emergency department if you have calf pain or swelling in one leg, or sudden shortness of breath or chest pain, which can be signs of a blood clot.
